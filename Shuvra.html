Charge framing hearing in fraud case against Evaly’s CEO on Jan 22

Star Digital Report

Sun Nov 27, 2022 01:09 PM Last update on: Sun Nov 27, 2022 01:14 PM

Evaly CEO Mohammad Rassel, and his wife and company chairman, Shamima Nasrin, must be made to give up all the money they stole from their customers and vendors. Photo: CollectedEvaly CEO Mohammad Rassel, and his wife and company chairman, Shamima Nasrin, must be made to give up all the money they stole from their customers and vendors. Photo: Collected

A Dhaka court today fixed January 22, 2023 for hearing on charge framing against e-commerce platform Evaly's Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer Mohammad Rassel and two others in a fraud case. The two other accused are Rassel's wife Shamima Nasrin, also chairman of the e-commerce platform, and its vice-president Abdullah Al Mamun alias Akash. Of them, Rassel is now behind bars, while Shamima and Akash are now on bail. Dhaka Metropolitan Magistrate Farah Diba Chhanda passed the order after Akash's lawyer submitted an application, seeking an adjournment of the hearing. Sub-Inspector Md Anisur Rahman of Dhanmondi Police Station, also the investigation officer of the case, on September 10 submitted the charge sheet to the Dhaka Chief Metropolitan Magistrate's Court. The IO dropped names of three others--Evaly's manager Jahedul Islam Hemoy, its high official Nasir Ahmed and bike department officer Sakib Rahman--from the charge sheet as their correct addresses were not found. On October 7 last year, Monir Hossain of Uttara Paschim, filed a complaint case with the CMM Court against Rassel, Shamima and four others on charges of misappropriating Tk, 2,36000. The case was registered with Dhanmondi Police Station on October 17 the same year following the court order.

BGB seizes 9 gold bars weighing 1kg from rickshaw van in Benapole

Star Digital Report

Sun Nov 27, 2022 11:07 AM Last update on: Sun Nov 27, 2022 11:11 AM

Members of Border Guard Bangladesh recovered nine gold bars weighing around 1kg from a passenger van at Benapole early today. The gold bars were recovered near Amrakhali BGB check post in Benapole around 12:30am today, reports our local correspondent. Jashore 49 BGB Battalion Captain Lt Col Shahed Minhaj Siddiqui said, "We came to know that gold bars were being smuggled into India after being hidden in a three-wheeler van. When the BGB members raided the Amrakhali area, nine gold bars were recovered from Milon alias Choto Babu's van. Milon left his van and fled. "A case has been filed against the driver for his alleged involvement in gold smuggling," he added.
